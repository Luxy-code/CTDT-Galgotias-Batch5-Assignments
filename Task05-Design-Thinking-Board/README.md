## 🪑 Product Concept: Smart Posture-Correcting Chair

### 1️⃣ The Ambiguous Problem
Office workers experience chronic back pain but frequently abandon wearable posture correctors because they are uncomfortable, aesthetically unappealing, and buzz aggressively during important meetings. The problem is not that people don't want good posture; the problem is that the delivery mechanism is highly intrusive.

### 2️⃣ The 5-Stage Design Thinking Solution

#### Step 1: Empathize
- **Observation:** People slump forward towards their monitors after exactly 2 hours of deep work.
- **Insight:** The solution must be invisible and require zero daily setup or straps from the user.

#### Step 2: Define
- **Target Persona:** Remote tech workers sitting 8+ hours a day.
- **HMW Statement:** *"How might we integrate posture correction seamlessly into the office environment so the user doesn't have to wear or remember anything?"*

#### Step 3: Ideate
- Brainstormed ideas included standing desks, camera-based tracking, and specialized chairs.
- **Winning Concept:** A smart chair with pressure sensors embedded in the backrest that gently nudges the lumbar support forward when it detects slouching.

#### Step 4: Prototype
- **MVP Build:** Modified a standard ergonomic chair by wiring pressure pads into the back mesh. Connected an Arduino to trigger a small vibration motor if the upper sensors lose contact for more than 5 minutes.
- **App Mockup:** A simple dashboard showing "Hours Sat Correctly" and "Slouch Count".

#### Step 5: Test
- **Feedback:** Users loved not wearing a harness and reported reduced back pain. However, they found the vibration during Zoom calls panic-inducing.
- **Iteration:** Updated the software to sync with Google Calendar/Outlook. The chair enters "Silent Mode" during meetings and uses a gentle desktop light cue instead of vibration.
