## 📄 Inference Report

### 1️⃣ Selected Problem Area
**Predictive Maintenance for Fluid / Rotating Machinery**

### 2️⃣ Reference Article
*Title:* **"Implementation of Digital Twin Technology for Predictive Maintenance in Industrial Rotating Machinery"**  
*Source:* Journal of Smart Manufacturing (2025)

### 3️⃣ Summary of the Article
The article discusses the transition from traditional scheduled (preventive) maintenance to condition-based (predictive) maintenance in power plants. By attaching IoT vibration and temperature sensors to rotating machines (like turbines and heavy-duty motors), continuous data is streamed to a "Digital Twin" — a virtual replica of the physical machine. Machine learning algorithms analyze this data to predict mechanical failures up to 4 weeks before they happen. 

### 4️⃣ Key Findings
- **High Downtime Costs:** Unexpected failures in rotating machinery contribute to 45% of total plant downtime.
- **Early Detection:** IoT sensors successfully identified bearing degradation 28 days before catastrophic failure.
- **Cost Reduction:** Implementing Digital Twin technology reduced overall maintenance costs by 30% by eliminating unnecessary scheduled repairs and preventing total machine loss.

### 5️⃣ Final Inference
The core inference drawn from this article is that **Digital Twin technology is no longer just a theoretical concept, but an operational necessity** for modern industrial plants. Shifting from "preventive" to "predictive" maintenance fundamentally transforms industrial economics by maximizing uptime and drastically reducing catastrophic equipment loss.
