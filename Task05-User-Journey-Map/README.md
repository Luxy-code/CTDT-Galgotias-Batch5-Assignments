## 🧭 Journey Map Breakdown: Samsung AI Family Hub

The User Journey Map explores how a user interacts with the Samsung AI Fridge across four distinct stages, mapping their actions, the AI's autonomous responses, and the user's emotional state.

### 1️⃣ Setup & Discovery
- **User Action:** Connects the fridge to Wi-Fi, creates a profile, and inputs dietary preferences.
- **AI Action:** Syncs with the Samsung Food app and calibrates internal cameras.
- **Emotion:** Curious but slightly overwhelmed by the 32-inch screen.
- **Opportunity:** Gamify the onboarding process with a quick interactive tutorial.

### 2️⃣ Stocking Groceries
- **User Action:** Returns from the supermarket, places items inside, and closes the door.
- **AI Action:** "Vision AI" takes photos, identifies up to 33 fresh food items, and auto-logs expiration dates.
- **Emotion:** Impressed, though slightly skeptical of how well it handles niche brands.
- **Opportunity:** Improve Vision AI datasets to recognize local or obscure packaging.

### 3️⃣ Meal Prep & Daily Use
- **User Action:** Opens the Samsung Food app, asks "What can I cook?", and views the recipe on the screen.
- **AI Action:** Cross-references inventory with user diet (e.g., gluten-free) and suggests customized recipes. Sends prep instructions to the Samsung oven.
- **Emotion:** Relieved and grateful for not having to think about dinner.
- **Opportunity:** Partner with fitness apps (like MyFitnessPal) to suggest meals based on daily caloric burn.

### 4️⃣ Depletion & Restock
- **User Action:** Uses the last of the milk, checks phone at work, and approves the grocery order.
- **AI Action:** Detects missing/expired items, sends a push notification, and generates an auto-shopping list via Amazon Fresh.
- **Emotion:** Delighted and feeling highly efficient.
- **Opportunity:** Enable seamless one-click purchasing directly from the fridge screen.
