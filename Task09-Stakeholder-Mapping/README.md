## 🏙️ Stakeholder Map Breakdown

For the initiative to transform a Smart City to strictly use E-Vehicles, we have analyzed the stakeholders using a standard **2x2 Power/Interest Grid**.

### 1️⃣ Manage Closely (High Power, High Interest)
These are the key players who can make or break the initiative. They require constant engagement.
- **City Mayor & Local Government Regulators:** Hold legislative power to mandate EV-only zones.
- **EV Infrastructure Providers (Tesla, ChargePoint):** Crucial for deploying city-wide charging grids.
- **Major Automotive EV Manufacturers:** Key suppliers and strategic partners.

### 2️⃣ Keep Satisfied (High Power, Low Interest)
These stakeholders have significant influence but aren't actively driving the EV initiative. They must be managed so they don't become roadblocks.
- **National Electricity Board:** Have the power to block expansion if the grid cannot handle the increased load.
- **Traditional Oil & Gas Corporations:** May resist the change and need strategic management.
- **Heavy Trucking / Logistics Companies:** Crucial to city economy; transitioning their fleets is difficult.

### 3️⃣ Keep Informed (Low Power, High Interest)
These groups care deeply about the project but lack direct power to change the policy.
- **Daily Commuters & City Residents:** Directly affected by the transition.
- **Environmental NGOs & Climate Activists:** Strong supporters who can provide positive PR.
- **Local EV Dealerships:** Highly interested in the economic boost.

### 4️⃣ Monitor (Low Power, Low Interest)
These stakeholders require minimum effort but should be monitored in case their status changes.
- **Peripheral Businesses (Gas stations, traditional mechanics):** Will slowly phase out or need to pivot their business models.
- **General Public from Neighboring Cities:** Not directly affected but may visit the city.
