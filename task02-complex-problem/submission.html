Healthcare
Biomedical Engineering
Drug-Resistant Bacterial Infections in ICU Patients
Rising antimicrobial resistance (AMR) in ICUs leads to untreatable infections, causing 700,000+ deaths/year globally. Standard antibiotics are failing due to bacterial mutation and overuse.
AMR is classified as one of the top 10 global public health threats by WHO (2019). The complexity arises from multi-factorial causes including misuse of antibiotics, lack of new drug pipelines, and cross-infection in hospital environments. Design Thinking can reframe the problem from treatment to prevention through smart diagnostic tools, stewardship protocols, and AI-driven drug discovery platforms.
📖 Source: WHO Antimicrobial Resistance Report 2019; Laxminarayan et al., The Lancet (2013)
2
Renewable Energy
Environmental Engineering
Energy Storage Limitation of Solar Power for Grid Stability
Solar energy generation is intermittent — it produces power only during daylight. Without cost-effective, large-scale storage, excess solar energy is wasted while grids remain unstable at night or during cloudy periods.
The International Energy Agency (IEA) reports that grid-scale storage is the biggest barrier to 100% renewable energy adoption. Current lithium-ion battery technology is expensive, resource-intensive, and has limited cycle life. The complexity involves electrochemical engineering, grid management, economic viability, and policy alignment — a true multi-disciplinary challenge requiring Design Thinking for system-level innovation.
📖 Source: IEA World Energy Outlook 2023; Dunn et al., Science (2011)
3
Manufacturing
Industry 4.0 / Smart Manufacturing
Unplanned Downtime in CNC Machines due to Unpredictable Failure
In large-scale manufacturing plants, CNC machine failures cause unplanned downtime costing up to $260,000/hour (Aberdeen Group). Root causes include tool wear, spindle failure, and thermal deformation — all difficult to predict with conventional monitoring.
McKinsey (2022) estimates that predictive maintenance can reduce machine downtime by 30–50% and maintenance costs by 25%. However, implementation is complex due to heterogeneous sensor data, legacy machine infrastructure, and lack of standardized AI models. Design Thinking helps re-engineer maintenance workflows — shifting from reactive to predictive paradigms — through IoT integration, digital twin simulation, and human-centered alert dashboards for technicians.
📖 Source: McKinsey Industry 4.0 Report (2022); Tao et al., IEEE Access (2018)
4
Transportation
Urban Mobility / Logistics
Last-Mile Delivery Inefficiency in Dense Urban Areas
Last-mile delivery accounts for 53% of total shipping costs (Capgemini, 2019). Urban congestion, failed delivery attempts, and fragmented logistics networks create a complex, costly, and environmentally damaging final step in supply chains.
The last-mile problem is a complex systems challenge involving urban planning, consumer behavior, vehicle routing, and carbon emission constraints. Design Thinking reveals that the root cause is not just logistics but the mismatch between delivery windows and customer availability. Solutions like micro-fulfillment centers, drone delivery, and AI-powered route optimization require interdisciplinary collaboration across engineering, policy, and behavioral science.
📖 Source: Capgemini Research Institute (2019); Boysen et al., European Journal of OR (2021)
5
Agriculture
Food Technology / AgriTech
Post-Harvest Food Loss in Developing Nations
Approximately 14% of the world's food is lost between harvest and retail (FAO, 2019), causing $400 billion in annual losses. Poor cold chain infrastructure, inadequate storage, and lack of market linkages are primary causes in developing countries like India.
Post-harvest loss is a wicked problem because it spans agronomy, cold chain logistics, market economics, and farmer literacy. The complexity is amplified by rural infrastructure gaps and climate variability. Design Thinking empathy mapping with smallholder farmers reveals that the actual user need is not just storage — but market access and fair pricing. Solutions must co-design cold storage cooperatives, mobile market platforms, and government policy reform simultaneously.
📖 Source: FAO Food Loss and Waste Report (2019); Kumar & Kalita, Sustainability MDPI (2017)

📌 Key Observations from Literature

  • Complex problems are multi-disciplinary — they span engineering, policy, economics, and human behavior simultaneously
  • Design Thinking uniquely addresses complex problems by starting with empathy — understanding the human at the center of the system
  • Literature across all 5 domains confirms that purely technical solutions fail without human-centered design and stakeholder co-creation
  • All identified problems are characterized by high uncertainty, multiple conflicting constraints, and no single "correct" solution — hallmarks of true complexity
  • Iterative prototyping (core to Design Thinking) allows low-cost testing of solutions before large-scale investment
